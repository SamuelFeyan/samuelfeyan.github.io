About Me

I'm an undergraduate physics major at Clemson University whose interests lie in multi-messenger astrophysics, specifically compact astrophysical binaries and their associated electromagnetic and gravitational wave emissions. I study massive black hole binary inspirals embedded in accretion disks to probe their coupled interactions and how they can lead to electromagnetic and gravitational wave observables. I utilize our Clemson research group's GPU-accelerated hydrodynamics code Sailfish and run simulations on Clemson's Palmetto compute cluster.
